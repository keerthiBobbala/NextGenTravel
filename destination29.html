West Bengal - Kolkata

Overview

West Bengal, located on the eastern coast of India, is a vibrant state known for its rich cultural heritage, diverse landscapes, and historical significance. From the bustling streets of Kolkata to the tranquil beauty of the Sundarbans, it offers a unique blend of tradition and modernity.

Attractions

Victoria Memorial, Kolkata

A grand historical monument and museum showcasing the British colonial era and offering stunning architecture and gardens.

Sundarbans National Park

The largest mangrove forest in the world, known for its rich biodiversity, including the Bengal tiger.

Activities

  • Boat Ride in the Sundarbans: Explore the mangrove forests and spot wildlife, including the elusive Bengal tiger.

  • Visiting the Kalighat Temple: Experience spiritual serenity at one of the holiest temples in Kolkata.

  • River Cruise on the Hooghly River: Enjoy the beautiful skyline of Kolkata while cruising on the Hooghly.

Accommodation

Here are some recommended places to stay:

  • The Oberoi Grand, Kolkata -A luxurious heritage hotel offering impeccable service and colonial charm in the heart of Kolkata.

  • Taj Bengal, Kolkata -A five-star hotel known for its blend of traditional hospitality and modern amenities.

  • Sundarbans Jungle Camp - An eco-friendly resort offering an immersive experience in the heart of the Sundarbans mangrove forest.

  • The Park, Kolkata -A modern luxury hotel with stylish interiors, located in the city’s vibrant cultural center.

  • Royal Sarovar Portico, Durgapur - A contemporary hotel offering a comfortable stay with excellent dining and amenities.

Travel Tips

  • Best Time to Visit - October to March and April to June and July to September.

  • Respect the Environment - Dispose of waste properly and avoid littering in natural areas.

  • Safety - Keep your belongings secure and be cautious of pickpockets in crowded places.

Transportation

  • By Air - The main airport is Netaji Subhas Chandra Bose International Airport in Kolkata, offering domestic and international flights.

  • By Train - West Bengal is well-connected by rail, with major stations like Howrah Junction and Sealdah in Kolkata linking the state to other parts of India.

  • By Bus - State-run and private buses connect Kolkata and other cities in West Bengal to neighboring states and destinations.

  • By Taxi - Taxis and auto-rickshaws are widely available for local travel within cities like Kolkata, Darjeeling, and Siliguri.
