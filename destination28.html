Uttarkhand-Nainital

Overview

Uttarakhand, nestled in the Himalayas, is a breathtaking blend of spiritual sanctity and natural beauty. Known as “Devbhumi” (Land of the Gods), it offers sacred shrines, serene hill stations, and thrilling adventure spots.

Attractions

Kedarnath Temple

A sacred Himalayan shrine dedicated to Lord Shiva, nestled amidst snow-capped peaks.

Nainital Lake

A picturesque, crescent-shaped lake surrounded by lush hills, perfect for boating and nature walks.

Activities

  • Trekking in Valley of Flowers: Hike through a UNESCO World Heritage Site bursting with alpine blooms and scenic beauty.

  • Paragliding in Nainital:Soar high above the beautiful lakes and hills, enjoying a bird's-eye view of the landscape.

  • Rafting in Rishikesh: Experience the thrill of rafting on the Ganges with its varying levels of rapids.

Accommodation

Here are some recommended places to stay:

  • Aqua Terra, Rishikesh -A luxury resort offering stunning views of the Ganges River, ideal for relaxation and adventure.

  • The Westin, Sohna -A luxurious retreat offering a perfect blend of nature and comfort in the serene hills of Uttarakhand.

  • Ananda in the Himalayas, Narendra Nagar - A world-renowned wellness retreat offering spa therapies and breathtaking Himalayan views.

  • Jim Corbett Resort, Jim Corbett National Park - A peaceful resort near the park, perfect for wildlife enthusiasts and nature lovers.

  • Naini Retreat, Nainital - A heritage hotel offering stunning views of Nainital Lake and the surrounding hills.

Travel Tips

  • Best Time to Visit - March to June and September to November.

  • Respect the Environment - Dispose of waste properly and avoid littering in natural areas.

  • Safety - Keep your belongings secure and be cautious of pickpockets in crowded places.

Transportation

  • By Air - The state has several airports, with Dehradun's Jolly Grant Airport being the main one, offering connections to major cities like Delhi and Mumbai.

  • By Train - Uttarakhand is well-connected by rail, with major stations like Haridwar, Rishikesh, and Dehradun serving as key entry points.

  • By Bus - Both state-run and private buses connect Uttarakhand with neighboring states, with services to cities like Delhi, Lucknow, and Chandigarh.

  • By Taxi - Taxis are widely available for intercity and local travel, offering flexibility for tourists to explore remote areas.
