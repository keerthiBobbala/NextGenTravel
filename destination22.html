Nagaland-Kohima

Overview

Nagaland, the land of festivals, mesmerizes with its vibrant tribal culture, rolling hills, and breathtaking landscapes.

Attractions

Dzukou Valley

A breathtaking valley known for its rolling green hills, seasonal flowers, and serene trekking trails.

Kohima War Cemetery

A historic memorial honoring soldiers of World War II, set amidst a peaceful hillside with panoramic views.

Activities

  • Trek to Dzukou Valley: Experience a scenic trek through lush green hills and vibrant seasonal flowers.

  • Explore Kohima War Cemetery:Pay tribute to World War II soldiers while enjoying panoramic city views.

  • Attend the Hornbill Festival: Witness Nagaland’s biggest cultural festival with traditional dances, music, and indigenous crafts.

Accommodation

Here are some recommended places to stay:

  • The Heritage, Kohima - A charming colonial-style hotel offering cozy rooms and easy access to major attractions.

  • Japfu Ashok, Kohima -A well-rated hotel with modern amenities, great hospitality, and scenic hilltop views.

  • Hotel Vivor, Kohima - A comfortable stay option with spacious rooms, an in-house restaurant, and excellent service.

  • The Naga Homestay, Khonoma - A traditional homestay experience with warm hospitality and insights into Naga culture.

  • Noune Resort, Dimapur - A peaceful lakeside resort offering cottages, adventure activities, and stunning natural surroundings.

Travel Tips

  • Best Time to Visit - The ideal time to visit Nagaland is between October and May, with December being the best month to experience the Hornbill Festival.

  • Explore Nearby Attractions - Discover stunning landscapes, cultural heritage sites, and hidden gems around your location for an enriching travel experience.

  • Cash and ATMs - Carry some cash as not all places accept cards, and ATMs can be sparse in remote areas.

  • Respect the Environment - Dispose of waste properly and avoid littering in natural areas.

  • Safety - Keep your belongings secure and be cautious of pickpockets in crowded places.

Transportation

  • By Road - Nagaland is connected to neighboring states via NH-29, linking Dimapur to Kohima and further to Manipur. Buses, shared sumos, and private taxis operate between major towns and cities.

  • Railways - Dimapur Railway Station is the major railhead in Nagaland, well-connected to cities like Guwahati, Kolkata, and Delhi. From Dimapur, travelers can take taxis or buses to other parts of the state.

  • Air Travel - Dimapur Airport is the only airport in Nagaland, with regular flights connecting to cities like Kolkata and Guwahati. From Dimapur, taxis and buses are available to reach Kohima and other destinations

  • Private Transport - The most common mode of transport in Kohima and Dimapur; available for local sightseeing and intercity travel.
